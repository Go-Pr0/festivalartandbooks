Review of The Inheritance, by Simon Tolkien

Minotaur Books, 2010 - ISBN 978-0-312-53907-8

This is the second book by the grandson of J.R.R. Tolkien, and can be termed a courtroom thriller as was Final Witness his first novel, but that would be to miss the nuances and the sheer breadth of the work. This novel is set in Oxfordshire, and evokes the landscape of both Britain and France in the post-war years very effectively. What I love about Simon Tolkien’s writing is that he develops some wonderfully believable characters in a complex thriller that keeps you guessing right to the end. His world-weary detective seems to evoke Colin Dexter’s Morse at times, though this is a writer with his own unique vision and voice. Add to the normal courtroom thriller and murder mystery an ancient artefact whose possession has led to war crimes and murder, and you have the recipe for a seat of your pants journey in motive and plot-twist. The action is well paced and assuredly well handled. The ending gives scope for the reader’s imagination too. In a word - brilliant. There is much more to come from Simon Tolkien’s pen, and I for one can’t wait to read it.

Alex Lewis.
